
# Starbucks vs Dunkins — Financial Analysis

This repository contains a group financial analysis comparing Starbucks and Dunkin'. It includes the final PDF report and the Excel workbook used for calculations and tables.

**View the interactive analysis online:** [https://bchopra785.github.io/starbucks-dunkin-financial-analysis/](https://bchopra785.github.io/starbucks-dunkin-financial-analysis/)

Project objective
- Compare financial performance and position of Starbucks and Dunkin' using common-size statements, cash flow analysis, and DuPont decomposition. Identify drivers of ROE/ROA and highlight material differences in margins, asset utilization, and leverage.

Files
- `Starbucks vs. Dunkins - Report.pdf` — final report summarizing methodology, analysis, findings, and recommendations.
- `Starbucks vs. Dunkins Project - Statements (etc).xlsx` — source workbook. Key sheets listed below.
- `docs/` — interactive HTML sheets and the website index for GitHub Pages (see link above)

Methodology (summary)
- Common-size financial statements: convert income statement and balance sheet line items to percentages of sales and assets to enable direct comparison across firms and periods.
- DuPont decomposition: break down Return on Equity (ROE) into Profit Margin, Asset Turnover, and Financial Leverage to identify which components drive differences between Starbucks and Dunkin'.
- Cash flow analysis: analyze operating, investing, and financing cash flows to assess sustainability and capital allocation.

Key findings (extracted from the report)
- Dunkin' reported systemwide sales growth of approximately 7.6% in the analyzed period and total revenues increased by about $48.6 million (≈3.7%), driven by franchise/royalty income, rental income, and advertising fees.
- Profit margins (selected years): Starbucks' profit margin decreased from ~18.28% (2018) to ~13.58% (2019), while Dunkin's margin increased slightly from ~17.40% to ~17.66% over the same period.
- Asset turnover: Starbucks' asset turnover fell from ~1.73 (2018) to ~1.22 (2019); Dunkin's rose slightly from ~0.36 to ~0.37, indicating different trends in asset utilization.
- Financial leverage: Starbucks' reported a large decrease in leverage (from ~4.30 to ~-8.58 in the sampled years), and Dunkin' also reduced leverage (from ~-10.50 to ~-5.67), reflecting deleveraging or changes in equity accounting in the period analyzed.

Notes on numbers
- The percentages and values above were taken directly from the report's DuPont and ratio analysis sections. Refer to the PDF for full tables, context, and appendices with calculations.
